## Overview

ChordForge can be used to create chord diagrams, press positions, and tutorial diagrams for stringed instruments (such as guitar, ukulele, and bass) for versatile purposes. One example is for editorial needs by simplifying the process of creating these diagrams, and offering various ways to obtain them, such as SVG, PNG, JPG, or even simply copying and pasting them directly into your editor tools.

### Semi-Realistic Scale

Diagrams are created to resemble the actual size of the instrument, including scale (nut to bridge), fret spacing, initial neck width, and final neck width. You can adjust all of these as you like.

### Fairly Customizable

Adjust the diagram to your liking, for example, by changing the instrument, scale length, neck width, marker size, font & size, and more.


## How to Use

1. Select an instrument

2. Click on the diagram to mark the finger positions as desired

    - Click before the nut to mark a muted string or open string
    - Click anywhere on the fretboard to mark the finger position

   or you can start by selecting a chord and its variations, then adjust the positions.

3. Customize the diagram to your liking.

4. Export the result. You can adjust the image width. You can choose SVG, PNG, JPG formats, or simply copy it if you don't want to bother downloading it.
